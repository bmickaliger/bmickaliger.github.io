Science Communication:
  • Public Talks: As an astronomy educator at the Vanderbilt Planetarium, I regularly give live presentations to audiences of up to 140 people. These presentations range from 15 to 45 minutes, and include speaking about a given topic over a microphone while also running a visual presentation to accompany my voice. The visuals are impressive, utilizing the full-dome capabilities of the planetarium software to explore dark skies not visible from Long Island, to full-dome projections of HST and JWST images, to 3D simulations of galactic surveys to explore the furthest reaches of the universe. While controlling these visuals, I also speak to them in a way that is easily understood by the general public with little to no prior knowledge of astronomy, while also being appropriate for people of all ages. The process includes a lot of multitasking and prior preparation to ensure that these live talks are accurate, engaging, comprehensive, and cohesive.
